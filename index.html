Together as One


Seattle, Washington. The Emerald City. The city where you can climb a mountain, kayak a river and eat an upscale dinner all within minutes of each other. The city that brought our hearts together.

For as long as I can remember, I’ve longed to witness Seattle and Washington’s natural beauty with my own two eyes. So, it was nothing less than a beautiful act of God that Matt asked me to accompany him on the trip only two months after we started dating. Little did I know, we would gain more than just fresh mountain air and the mass existence of trees. We gained a new beginning; for a love that has grown stronger with each passing day.

Over the next few months of our relationship, our life became one big, shared adventure. We took advantage of as many little “adventures” as our schedules would allow. It was those spontaneous getaways that we attribute to falling more in love with each other. These times were guaranteed weekends of laughs, fishing until our backs were sore, pigging out on bagels, communicating in goofy accents, and racking up free room nights on hotels.com. Not to mention, Matt quickly accepting my addiction for a daily Starbucks and my humoring all of his “punny” jokes (Ok, they really are hilarious!) We found unity in our adventurous hearts.

God had a glorious plan. One that would grant me my long, sought-after life-journey buddy. Our love had begun at one end of the country, and was about to open a new chapter at the opposite end entirely. The East. New York City.

Giddy as could be, I couldn’t believe I was going to get to make the trip out to NYC all by myself to meet up with Matt as he finished up a business trip. I figured I was brave enough to hail a taxi in the big city if I could take on the Dallas North Tollway traffic (yeah right!) New York City was experiencing a heat wave that even us Texans had a hard time adjusting to.

All of the factors surrounding us would have sent anyone into a state of panic, but I’ve never seen a more calm and collected man. Through the heat, crowds of people, and honking cars, he pulled me. Out of the typical hustle and bustle and into an urban oasis: Central Park. A serene gondola ride in the lake lead to a quick stroll around famous spots in the park, including a particular bridge. There, the stage was set. The people perfectly scattered, as if for our special moment. The next image will be engrained in my mind forever. I said yes.
